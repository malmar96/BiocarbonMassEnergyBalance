Model Assumptions & Limitations
— Biomass elemental composition modelled as C, H, O only. N and S neglected (small for woody biomass; more significant for sludge).
— Ash is assumed inert and exits entirely with biochar. In practice some ash volatilises or is entrained in exhaust gas.
— Pyrolysis modelled as a single endothermic step at T_reactor. Multi-stage kinetics and tar cracking not included.
— Syngas is assumed completely combusted in the thermal oxidiser at 100% efficiency. Partial combustion losses are not modelled.
— Acid dew point fixed at 150°C. For high-sulphur feedstocks (e.g. sludge) the actual dew point may be considerably higher.
— HHV biochar is entered on an ash-free (organic fraction) basis. Typical char values: 28,000–32,000 kJ/kg organic.
— Excess O₂ target is specified as a dry molar fraction, consistent with standard flue gas analysers (e.g. Testo, Wohler).
— Combustion floor temperature (default 650°C) is an engineering judgement; actual minimum depends on syngas composition and TO design.
